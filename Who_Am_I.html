Who Am I?

My name is Kennedy Nguyen. I do have a middle name, but I prefer not to use it as much as I can. I'll skip my grade school years. I mean, who really cares in this time where people want immediate answers. Google is at our fingertips when we need something reasonable. And there isn't much in my childhood.
My academic career began when I started at East Los Angeles College. I originally was an undecided major who later leaned towards history and philosophy. Then I jumped over to mathematics and programming. I was a terrible programmer when I started. The first language I started to understand programming was C++. Then I jumped over to physics. Initially, I didn't like physics, but that changed when I met a tough but caring professor. I pretty much took enough classes to get associates degrees for mathematics, philosophy, physics, and computer science. Emphasis on for since programs for the latter 3 subjects didn't not exist until after I transferred. And I never bothered with the mathematics one.
When I transferred to CSULA, I convinced myself to get my bachelors degree within two years. My first year at CSULA covered a lot of what I know now. Including my knowledge of HTML and CSS you're reading here. Though I'll admit I'm still relearning with help from a friend. I still feel like my decision to pick all the electives that I thought were useful in my second year was a mistake. All of my electives were considered hard electives for senior students, and many of them were prerequisites for graduate level studies. After my grades were finalized for graduation, I didn't expect to get degree honors. I also felt that turning down the opportunity to advance into CSULA's graduate program was another mistake I made. Oh, well... Despite my terrible life decisions, my senior year was probably the most interesting year during my stay.
But I personally wanted to work for while before considering graduate school.

My Curiousity and Where It's Leading Me

I think I'm pathetic that I know more programming languages than human languages. Despite my surname, I am terrible at Vietnamese. And despite my programming history that I wrote below, I still don't trust myself to consider myself a good programmer.
I chose computer science because I hoped for a lasting career that makes me useful, but the field also encompassed everything I'm interested in. Video games, games, applied logic, audio editing, audio mixing, applied math, graphics and graphical editing, data analysis, applied physics, and not having to lift physically heavy objects or deal with physical mechanical problems I am terrible at. Being honest, I was going to give up back in 2018. My family had wanted to throw me out. My classes didn't amount to any associates at the time despite being over the units for a community college student. My close relatives wanted me to get an associate's degree and stop there. My old college friend, David Holmes, was pretty much my shoulder to cry on for days at that time. My degree now amounts to being meaningless... But yeah, I really appreciate him for changing my mind and keeping my sanity. Bootleg Programmer
I've been programming in C++ since 2013, and I only recently realized that I've been using it for as long as I've had my California driver's license. Java was my second programming language. I took a class in Java in 2012, and never touched it again until I transferred into CSULA. Though, I haven't touched it again after graduation. I have used HTML, JavaScript, and CSS on and off since my web development class at CSULA. I used to hate web development, mainly because of the professor I had. But recent projects I've worked with, and help from a friend who's emphasis was mobile development, changed my mind.I have been playing with Python since 2018, but I feel like I've learned much more during senior year. My senior year was probably the most interesting because I took some of the hardest electives, and I actually liked my capstone project that involved being a year-long intern for Vodafone. I felt I learned more Python (through jupyter notebook) during my data science and machine learning classes, and my internship with Vodafone. I mainly worked on using Python during my internship, but I used to use Tableau and Javascript as well. This might surprise you, but I don't really write "pythonic" code in python. I've learned SQL during my stay at CSULA, and a recent project forced me to learn accessing a relational database through Python. Before, I used to think that machine learning was more for forecasting or to make things "more intelligent". But I learned that I could do visualizations, find the more useful parts of data, use machine learning algorithms to prepare data that are to be fed into more machine learning algorithms, and, what caught my interest, finding useful information that are the least obvious.
